# Apartment 302 False-Lead Balance Guide

Use this note during room-dressing or screenshot review when the mirror trap, window beat, or pawn pouch start competing too strongly with the nursery answer path.

This is a laptop 2 readability guide only. It does not change clue logic, prompts, or interaction range.

## Goal

Keep the room suspicious and layered without letting any false lead read as the clearest first answer from entry.

## Intended salience order

From the apartment entry and first nursery approach, the room should usually read in this order:

1. nursery clue family
2. correct nursery sigil / answer line
3. mirror trap as a tempting but secondary pull
4. window latch beat
5. pawn pouch beat

If the order flips, the room starts teaching the wrong solution.

## False-lead targets

### Mirror trap

Keep:

- visibly strange
- readable on a second sweep
- strong enough to reward curiosity after the nursery read

Avoid:

- the brightest highlight in the room
- the cleanest silhouette from entry
- a single uninterrupted sightline from the door to the mirror circle

### Window latch beat

Keep:

- local and plausible
- readable as a domestic disturbance
- weaker than the nursery clue family in both contrast and shape clarity

Avoid:

- a bright edge light that beats the nursery cluster
- curtains or blinds that form a stronger frame than the crib area
- clutter that points the eye straight to the window before the nursery sweep lands

### Pawn pouch beat

Keep:

- low and discoverable on approach
- distinct enough to feel intentional once the player explores

Avoid:

- oversized placement near entry sightlines
- clean isolation against a bright floor patch
- pairing it too tightly with the window beat so both false leads read as one obvious answer zone

## Fast review questions

1. Does the room still teach nursery first from the doorway?
2. Does the mirror trap feel tempting later instead of dominant immediately?
3. Did the window area gain stronger contrast than the crib cluster?
4. Did the pawn pouch become too easy to spot before the player reaches the nursery zone?
5. Do the three false-lead beats stay separate enough to feel like layered suspicion rather than one loud wrong answer?

## Safe fixes inside this lane

- lower contrast on mirror-adjacent props
- break the entry sightline into the mirror circle with softer clutter
- reduce curtain, window, or latch emphasis if it beats the nursery cluster
- push the pawn pouch slightly deeper into the approach path instead of the entry read
- separate false-lead props so each one supports misdirection without merging into a single visual wall

## Out of scope

- changing clue ownership
- changing prompt text
- changing ritual logic
- changing interaction range

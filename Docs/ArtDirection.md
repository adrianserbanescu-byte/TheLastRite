# Art Direction

## Goal

Aim for a **readable absurdist exorcism party game**:

- stylized 3D
- readable silhouettes
- exaggerated shapes
- occult logic played straight
- strong readability for both players and spectators

The game should feel like a haunted disaster a friend group can understand instantly and retell later.

## Commercial reference translation

Use **Totally Secure Airport** on Steam as the benchmark for the **level of readability, stylization, and commercial finish**, not as a theme copy.

What to carry forward from that reference:

- broad, immediately readable 3D shapes
- clean, deliberate materials instead of muddy realism
- strong prop readability from a thumbnail or stream view
- playful exaggeration in scale and silhouette
- a commercial co-op-sim art bar that reads fast in screenshots
- color separation that helps players parse roles, interactables, and state changes quickly

What to translate for The Last Rite:

- airport satire becomes occult apartment panic
- bright public-space clarity becomes haunted-room clarity
- comic civilian clutter becomes ritual, evidence, and bait readability
- toy-like object legibility becomes exorcism-tool and evidence legibility

What not to copy:

- airport theming
- flat comedy without stakes
- bright-for-the-sake-of-brightness palettes that break the occult tone

## Core rules

- stylized 3D, not realism-first horror
- clear silhouettes before fine detail
- chunky props and readable interaction targets
- commercial-quality readability over indie-horror grime
- clean materials with grime as accent, not blanket noise
- room-state changes must read in motion and in screenshots
- absurdity should come from physical breakdown, ritual mistakes, and possession-state reversals, not random clutter
- lighting must preserve object read; darkness is allowed, mud is not
- important props should read almost like co-op sim tools first and horror relics second

## Tone rules

The art should support:

- eerie setups
- sincere occult logic
- funny overcommitment
- visible ritual mistakes
- readable cause-and-effect
- friend-group storytelling

The art should avoid:

- muddy realism
- oppressive visual noise
- tiny important details hidden in dark corners
- generic grim horror palettes that flatten the game into category wallpaper
- prestige-horror overrendering that slows down room read
- texture density that hides evidence silhouettes

## Apartment 302 rules

Apartment 302 is one apartment that must support three demon reads:

- **Hollow Saint**: nursery truth, mirror bait
- **Weeping Matron**: mirror truth, nursery bait
- **The Appetite**: body area truth, ritual-area bait

This means the apartment art must support:

- stable geometry and layout
- shifting truth/bait interpretation by demon
- re-readable evidence clusters
- strong zone identity for player callouts
- a cleaner, more commercially legible object pass than the current gritty-slice default

The same room needs to tell three different stories without looking random.

## Ritual readability rules

The 4-hand ritual must be readable from multiple camera angles.

Art should help players and spectators answer:

- which phase the ritual is in
- where the Reader, Anchor, Censer, and Guard should be
- whether the circle is stable or inverted
- what just failed and why

This means:

- strong tool silhouettes
- readable ritual area composition
- clear phase escalation
- unmistakable circle inversion from white to red
- visible demon pressure at distance and close range
- spectator readability comparable to a strong co-op sim, even during chaos

## Possession-state rules

The wrong-anchor failure must create a strong visual state shift:

- circle inversion
- darkness and emergency contrast
- suspicion without over-explaining the possessed player
- enough clarity that teammates can react fast

The possession state should feel like a genre pivot while staying legible.

## Stream-readability rules

The visual language should protect:

- the first mirror-crack reveal
- the voice-pitch-drop possession moment
- the clean exorcism payoff

Every one of those beats needs composition and lighting support.

## Finish target

The finish target is:

- stylized commercial indie, not placeholder horror prototype
- readable enough that a new player understands the room from a short clip
- clean enough that Steam screenshots sell the game without heavy explanation
- exaggerated enough that blame, panic, and tool roles read at a glance

If a scene is atmospheric but harder to parse than the reference-level co-op-sim read, it is below target.

## Unreal carry-forward

When moving forward in Unreal, keep:

- strong silhouettes
- limited material noise
- distinct demon-facing accent colors and motif families
- readable ritual FX
- strong zone identity for callouts
- stream-friendly composition and contrast
